# NewsGPT: ChatGPT Integration for Robot-Reporter

\fnmAbdelhadi \surHireche <abdelhadi.h@uaeu.ac.ae>    <belkacem@uaeu.ac.ae>    \fnmSadia \surJamil <sadia.jamil@nottingham.edu.cn>    \fnmChao \surChen <chao_chen@email.tjut.edu.cn> \* \ \[ \[

###### Abstract

The integration of large language models (LLMs) with social robots has emerged as a promising avenue for enhancing human‒robot interactions at a time when news reports generated by artificial intelligence (AI) are gaining in credibility. This integration is expected to intensify and become a more productive resource for journalism, media, communication, and education. In this paper a novel system is proposed that integrates AI’s generative pretrained transformer (GPT) model with the Pepper robot, with the aim of improving the robot’s natural language understanding and response generation capabilities for enhanced social interactions. By leveraging GPT’s powerful language processing capabilities, this system offers a comprehensive pipeline that incorporates voice input recording, speech-to-text transcription, context analysis, and text-to-speech synthesis action generation. The Pepper robot is enabled to comprehend user queries, generate informative responses with general knowledge, maintain contextually relevant conversations, and act as a more domain-oriented news reporter. It is also linked with a news resource and powered with a Google search capability. To evaluate the performance of the framework, experiments were conducted involving a set of diverse questions. The robot’s responses were assessed on the basis of eight criteria, including relevance, context, and fluency. Despite some identified limitations, this system contributes to the field of journalism and human‒robot interaction by showcasing the potential of integrating LLMs with social robots. The proposed framework opens up opportunities for improving the conversational capabilities of robots, enabling interactions that are smoother, more engaging, and more context aware.

###### keywords:

Automated journalism, ChatGPT, NewsGPT, robot reporter, human‒robot interaction.

## 1 Introduction

The use of large language models with robots has advanced significantly in recent years, enabling robots to engage in more natural and context-aware interactions with humans \[[1\]. These language models, such as OpenAI’s GPT (generative pretrained transformer), have demonstrated remarkable capabilities in understanding and generating human-like text \[2, 3\]. A possible application of this integration is with the Pepper robot, a humanoid robot designed for social interactions and assistance in various domains.

The integration of ChatGPT with the Pepper robot represents a significant advance in human‒robot interaction, bridging the intriguing divide between humans and robots and approaching seamless engagement \[4\]. Merging the power of an advanced language model with a socially adept robot is leading the way to more intelligent, natural, and context-aware conversations.

One immediate application is in the area of news presentation and delivery. News reporting is experiencing a surge in credibility among audiences due to the incorporation of machine-generated news \[5\]. This has become an indispensable tool for journalists, serving as a vital resource for their productivity \[6\]. With the integration of ChatGPT, many research articles have investigated the creation of robotic software reporters \[7, 8, 9\], While this deal only with software aspects, the Pepper robot has the humanoid appearance and expressive gestures of a person, capable of reporting news and conveying information in a more engaging and natural manner. The advanced language model enables the robot to understand and summarize complex news articles and deliver live news updates quickly, providing the audience with concise and informative reports. As this technology continues to evolve, it holds the potential to transform the way we interact with robots, making them indispensable companions in various domains, including education, healthcare, and customer service \[10\].

AI-based robot reporters have the potential to replace human reporters for various compelling reasons:

1\. Speed and efficiency: ChatGPT-based robots can process vast amounts of information quickly and efficiently. They can rapidly analyze data, generate reports, and disseminate news in real time. This enables faster news delivery, keeping the audience up to date with the latest developments without delays caused by human limitations.

2\. Elimination of bias: Human reporters are susceptible to personal biases that can inadvertently affect their reporting. ChatGPT-based robots, being driven by algorithms, are less prone to bias and can provide a more objective representation of the facts. This ensures a fairer and more balanced dissemination of information.

3\. Consistency and accuracy: Human journalists may make errors or overlook critical details in their reporting. ChatGPT-based robots, in contrast, can provide consistent and accurate news coverage by relying on their vast knowledge base, fact-checking capabilities, and the ability to cross-reference information from various sources.

4\. Availability and scalability: Human reporters have limitations in terms of availability and scalability. They require breaks and sleep, and they cannot cover all news stories simultaneously. ChatGPT-based robots, however, can operate 24/7, tirelessly covering multiple stories simultaneously and ensuring a constant flow of news updates.

5\. Language adaptability: ChatGPT-based robots are designed to communicate in multiple languages. This allows them to provide news coverage and reports in different languages, making information more accessible to a global audience. Human reporters, on the other hand, may be limited by their language proficiency.

6\. Reduction in costs: Employing human reporters can be expensive, considering salaries, benefits, and training. ChatGPT-based robots offer a cost-effective alternative as they do not require the same level of compensation and can be easily scaled without incurring additional expenses.

7\. Safety and risk mitigation: Sending human reporters into dangerous or hazardous environments can pose significant risks to their safety. ChatGPT-based robots can be deployed to such locations, collecting real-time data and reporting without endangering human lives.

ChatGPT-based robot reporters can potentially assist human journalists greatly due to their speed, lack of bias, consistency, language adaptability, and cost effectiveness. While the integration of ChatGPT-based robots in reporting has its advantages, human reporters bring into play unique qualities such as emotional intelligence, intuition, and investigative skills. They also possess a deep understanding of societal nuances and can navigate complex ethical dilemmas more effectively. Striking a balance between the use of technology and the value of human journalism is crucial to ensure comprehensive and responsible news coverage. The ChatGPT-based robotic system proposed here can assist journalists by automating certain tasks such as live news reporting, but it cannot replace the invaluable contributions of human intelligence, intuition, empathy, and creativity that journalists bring to their work. The integration of chatbots and robots may enhance certain aspects of news reporting, but it cannot replace the vital role that human journalists play in society.

This paper presents a novel system that leverages the power of GPT integration with the Pepper robot to enhance social interactions and improve the robot’s ability to engage in meaningful conversations and to report news. The proposed framework explores the potential of combining the language generation capabilities of GPT with the physical embodiment and interactive features of the Pepper robot. By integrating these technologies, the aim is to create a more humanlike and engaging experience for users, narrowing the gap between humans and robots.

### 2.1 AI agent Tools

AI agents represent an emergent programming paradigm for various AI applications, ranging from question answering and AI management coaching to image generation services and AI companions.

The present study utilized a specific type of agent, known as the ReAct agent, which shares features of the original ReAct agent proposed by Shunyu Yao et al. This ReAct agent enables the generation of both reasoning traces and task-specific actions in an interleaved manner, promoting synergistic interactions between the two. The reasoning traces aid the model in deducing, tracking, and updating action plans, as well as handling exceptional scenarios \[11\].

To enrich the capabilities of the selected ReAct agent, certain tools were incorporated, such as the Facts Checker Tool, the News Tool and the Search Tool powered by SerpAPI from Google (https://serpapi.com/). These tools are selectively employed by the agent on the basis of the context and received prompts.

the News Tool utilizes the GNewsAPI (https://gnews.io/) to gather recent article headlines related to specific search terms provided by the model tested in this study. The response data retrieved from the News Tool is then utilized by the large language model to construct a news report, which is subsequently conveyed back to the user.

An additional tool is employed to enhance the verification process for checking the authenticity of facts and news. This tool utilizes a deep learning model that is trained on article titles, providing an additional layer of validation.

### 2.2 Fake News Detection using deep learning

To help the robot examine the news articles credibility, we follow an approach to classifying news articles as either fake or real based on their titles. The methodology involves multiple stages, beginning with data preprocessing where two distinct datasets containing fake and real news articles are use together, the dataset is available . each assigned a binary label ’0’ for fake and ’1’ for real news. To prepare the dataset for training, each title is truncated or padded to a uniform length, specified as buffer size, and is then transformed into an integer sequence using ASCII values.

The core of our approach lies in the neural network architecture employed for this classification task. The model adopts a sequential framework comprising an input layer to accommodate the preprocessed titles, followed by an embedding layer that maps the ASCII integers into a 64-dimensional space. Subsequently, a 1D convolutional layer with 32 filters and a kernel size of 5 is introduced to capture local sequence patterns. The model also incorporates a flatten layer and a fully connected dense layer with 128 neurons, both utilizing ReLU activation functions. The architecture culminates in an output layer with a single neuron employing a sigmoid activation function to yield probabilities suitable for binary classification.

For the compilation phase, the model is optimized using the adam algorithm and utilizes a binary cross-entropy loss function. Additionally, several evaluation metrics including accuracy, precision, recall, and Area Under the Curve (AUC) are employed to comprehensively assess the model’s performance. The model is trained over 10 epochs with a batch size of 64, using a stratified 80-20 train-validation split to maintain class balance.

#### 2.2.1 Automatic Speech Recognition

We utilized two different versions; the first is compatible with the native Nuance ASR that is pre-installed in the Pepper robot, designated for indoor environments where noise is very low. The second version is optimized for noisy conditions, making it suitable for outdoor environments.

The system is composed of two primary elements. Firstly, there’s the automatic speech detector, designed to identify the presence of speech by monitoring volume levels. This component initiates the recording process when sounds surpass a specified threshold. Secondly, the system employs the Whisper model, a sophisticated speech recognition service, to accurately interpret and transcribe the captured audio.

The semantic differential (SD) method is a method of measurement that uses subjective ratings of a concept or an object by means of scaling opposite adjectives to study connotative meaning of the concept or object. The technique involves presenting pairs of bipolar, or opposite, adjectives at either end of a series of scales. The difficult part about the semantic differential technique is coming up with words that are truly opposites.

### 2.4 Flow of Control

Evaluating a robot’s conversation involves assessing various aspects of its performance. Key factors to consider are as follows:

- •
  Relevance: This criterion measures how closely the robot’s responses align with the topic or question asked. It is evaluated by posing general questions, such as inquiring about a country’s capital, and assessing the relevance of the answers provided.
- •
  Context: This criterion assesses the robot’s ability to retain and recall information over the course of a conversation. It is evaluated by testing whether ChatGPT can remember previously provided information, such as the user’s name, after a certain period of time.
- •
  Bias: This criterion examines the presence of bias in the robot’s responses. It is evaluated by asking political questions or seeking opinions on controversial figures or topics.
- •
  Engaging conversation: This criterion measures the robot’s ability to maintain user interest and create engaging conversations. It takes into account factors such as the responsiveness, interactivity, and overall enjoyment of the conversation.
- •
  Fluency: This criterion evaluates the robot’s performance in terms of speech-to-text functionality, particularly focusing on the accuracy of transcription and the frequency of mistakes made during the conversation.
- •
  Error resilience: This criterion assesses the robot’s ability to handle and recover from unusual or unexpected inputs. It involves testing the robot’s response to unconventional or nonsensical words or phrases.
- •
  Domain orientation: This criterion examines the robot’s familiarity with specific domains and its ability to provide information within those domains. It is evaluated by asking the robot about the latest news in a particular country and testing its knowledge of specific past news events.
- •
  Response time: The robot’s response time is rated over a few different news-related questions and more general knowledge ones.
- •
  Satisfaction: This criterion measures the level of user satisfaction with the robot’s responses and overall conversation experience. It involves assessing whether the robot’s answers meet the user’s expectations, address their needs, and provide a positive interaction.
- •
  Creativity: This criterion evaluates the robot’s ability to generate novel and imaginative responses. It assesses whether the robot can offer unique perspectives, suggest creative solutions, or engage in imaginative discussions beyond providing factual information.

The numbered steps in Figure 4 signify the following:

1\. A server, hosted on a laptop, facilitates communication between a cloud-based AI agent, Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR), and the Pepper robot, while also managing chat logic.

2\. The Pepper robot, acting as the client, establishes a connection to the server. Once connected, the robot signals the commencement of listening, which is then relayed to the ASR system.

3\. Upon notification from the automatic speech detector, the ASR initiates the recording and decoding of received auditory input.

4\. If a continuous silence exceeding 2.5 seconds is detected, the automatic speech detector informs the server to cease recording. The server then procures the transcribed text from the ASR to forward to the cloud-hosted AI agent.

5\. Utilizing the GPT 3.5 model, among other tools, the AI agent formulates a response.

6\. This response is relayed by the server to the Pepper robot, which vocalizes the feedback to the user.

## 4 Conclusion

The integration of OpenAI’s GPT model with the Pepper robot presents exciting possibilities for human‒robot interaction and language capabilities. The NewsGPT system, showcased through the robot reporter, demonstrates the potential of using large language models in journalism. By leveraging GPT’s language processing capabilities, the Pepper robot can deliver news stories in a manner that is context aware and engaging. With the ability to comprehend complex articles and generate informative responses, the robot can provide live news updates and deliver concise reports to the audience. While the NewsGPT-based robot reporter offers advantages like speed, efficiency, and elimination of bias, it is important to acknowledge the unique qualities of human journalists, such as emotional intelligence and investigative skills, to ensure comprehensive and responsible news coverage. In conclusion, the NewsGPT framework detailed here represents a step forward in human‒robot interaction, bridging the gap between humans and robots in intelligent and interactive conversations. The integration of ChatGPT with the Pepper robot opens up opportunities for more engaging and context-aware interactions in various domains. However, it is crucial to strike a balance between the use of technology and the value of human journalism, as human reporters possess essential qualities such as intuition and understanding of societal nuances. As this technology continues to evolve, ethical considerations must be explored to ensure responsible use for the benefit of society as a whole in domains such as education, healthcare, and customer service.
