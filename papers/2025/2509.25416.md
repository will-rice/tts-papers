# Emotion-Aligned Generation in Diffusion Text to Speech Models via Preference-Guided Optimization

###### Abstract

Emotional text-to-speech seeks to convey affect while preserving intelligibility and prosody, yet existing methods rely on coarse labels or proxy classifiers and receive only utterance-level feedback. We introduce Emotion-Aware Stepwise Preference Optimization (EASPO), a post-training framework that aligns diffusion TTS with fine-grained emotional preferences at intermediate denoising steps. Central to our approach is EASPM, a time-conditioned model that scores noisy intermediate speech states and enables automatic preference pair construction. EASPO optimizes generation to match these stepwise preferences, enabling controllable emotional shaping. Experiments show superior performance over existing methods in both expressiveness and naturalness.

Index Terms—  Speech Synthesis, Diffusion Model, Emotional Text-To-Speech (Emo-TTS).

## 1 Introduction

Emotional text-to-speech (TTS) \[[2](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib1 "Fine-grained style control in transformer-based text-to-speech synthesis"), [10](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib2 "Prompttts: controllable text-to-speech with text descriptions"), [4](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib3 "Emospeech: guiding fastspeech2 towards emotional text to speech"), [9](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib4 "Emodiff: intensity controllable emotional text-to-speech with soft-label guidance"), [5](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib5 "Cosyvoice: a scalable multilingual zero-shot text-to-speech synthesizer based on supervised semantic tokens"), [6](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib6 "Cosyvoice 2: scalable streaming speech synthesis with large language models"), [19](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib7 "Emovoice: llm-based emotional text-to-speech model with freestyle text prompting")\] aims to generate speech that remains intelligible while conveying affect and prosodic nuance. Effective emotional control supports conversational agents, accessibility, and content creation. Yet fine-grained control without sacrificing naturalness remains challenging, as emotional cues unfold over time and interact with linguistic content and speaker variation.

Early emotional TTS systems fused local style tokens with phoneme content via cross-attention for fine-grained control \[[2](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib1 "Fine-grained style control in transformer-based text-to-speech synthesis")\], guided synthesis with semantic prompts \[[10](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib2 "Prompttts: controllable text-to-speech with text descriptions")\], and used variational decoders to model nuanced emotion–prosody relations \[[4](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib3 "Emospeech: guiding fastspeech2 towards emotional text to speech")\]. Diffusion-based methods improved fidelity and control through emotion embeddings and noise-conditioned prosody \[[9](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib4 "Emodiff: intensity controllable emotional text-to-speech with soft-label guidance")\]. Recent work adopts supervised speech tokens for semantic grounding and chunk-aware flow-based decoding \[[5](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib5 "Cosyvoice: a scalable multilingual zero-shot text-to-speech synthesizer based on supervised semantic tokens"), [6](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib6 "Cosyvoice 2: scalable streaming speech synthesis with large language models")\], and introduces parallel phoneme–audio branches in LLM-based generation to support fine-grained, freestyle emotional control \[[19](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib7 "Emovoice: llm-based emotional text-to-speech model with freestyle text prompting")\]. Across these variants, two important gaps remain: supervision still targets proxy labels over preference-driven prompts, and feedback remains temporally sparse, limiting constraints on prosody–emotion dynamics.

Direct Preference Optimization (DPO)\[[14](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib20 "Direct preference optimization: your language model is secretly a reward model")\] aligns generative models to human choices via paired comparisons against a frozen reference policy. In diffusion settings\[[18](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib10 "Diffusion model alignment using direct preference optimization")\], a common practice is to assign preference at the final step and propagate it to intermediate latents, then optimize a DPO-style log-likelihood ratio at each step. This removes the need for an explicit reward model and is effective for global preferences, but offers limited guidance for temporally evolving signals. DPO has also been used to align large language model–based TTS. EmoDPO \[[8](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib11 "Emo-dpo: controllable emotional speech synthesis through direct preference optimization")\], for instance, pairs utterances with identical text and marks the target emotion as preferred, achieving alignment without explicit reward modeling. However, attaching preference only at the endpoint yields sparse supervision for gradually varying cues, the assumption that all intermediate states on a preferred path are themselves preferred is often invalid, and curating domain-appropriate preference pairs is expensive. This leads to our central research question: How can we provide dense, fine-grained supervision for emotionally expressive TTS generation, without relying on endpoint preferences or categorical labels?

To better align diffusion-based TTS systems with fine-grained emotional preferences, we propose Emotion-Aware Stepwise Preference Optimization (EASPO). At each denoising step starting from a latent representation $`x_{t}`$, the model samples a small candidate set of $`x_{t-1}`$ mel-spectrograms. An Emotion-aware Stepwise Preference Model (EASPM) scores their emotional expressiveness and selects a win–lose pair that differs subtly in prosody while preserving linguistic content. One candidate is then randomly chosen to continue generation. Since these candidates originate from the same latent and differ by only a single denoising step, their variations are localized and emotion-focused. EASPM captures these nuanced differences and steers the model toward producing more emotionally consistent speech.

Our contributions are: 1) We propose EASPO, a stepwise alignment framework that reformulates preference optimization as a local, time-conditioned task, replacing the flawed assumption that all intermediate states on a preferred trajectory are equally valid. By aligning win/lose candidates at each denoising step from a shared latent, it enables stepwise-controllable emotion shaping throughout the generation process. 2) We introduce EASPM, a time-aware reward model that directly scores emotional expressiveness and prosody on noisy intermediate states, enabling dense, temporally grounded preference learning and on-the-fly scoring.

## 2 Method

We present a reinforcement learning framework for emotional TTS that fine-tunes diffusion models via stepwise preference supervision (Fig.[1](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#S1.F1 "Figure 1 ‣ 1 Introduction ‣ Emotion-Aligned Generation in Diffusion Text to Speech Models via Preference-Guided Optimization")). Starting from a pretrained Grad-TTS\[[13](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib22 "Grad-tts: a diffusion probabilistic model for text-to-speech")\], our method introduces dense emotion-aligned rewards at each denoising step. At every latent state, multiple mel-spectrograms are sampled and ranked by a frozen emotion preference model(Sec. [2.1](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#S2.SS1 "2.1 Emotion-Aware Stepwise Preference Model ‣ 2 Method ‣ Emotion-Aligned Generation in Diffusion Text to Speech Models via Preference-Guided Optimization")). A preference pair is selected, and the model is optimized to favor the emotionally preferred sample by minimizing the gap between its advantage and the log-likelihood ratio(Sec. [2.2](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#S2.SS2 "2.2 Objective Function of EASPO ‣ 2 Method ‣ Emotion-Aligned Generation in Diffusion Text to Speech Models via Preference-Guided Optimization")). Our EASPO objective extends DPO with stepwise preference signals, enabling fine-grained emotional control during generation.

### 2.1 Emotion-Aware Stepwise Preference Model

Overall. At reverse step $`t-1`$, given the current latent $`x_{t}`$, the generator draws $`k`$ candidates $`\{x_{t-1}^{1},\ldots,x_{t-1}^{k}\}\sim p_{\theta}(x_{t-1}\mid x_{t})`$. EASPM assigns each candidate a *timestep-aware* emotion–prompt consistency score and induces a ranking; the highest and lowest scored items are taken as the win/lose pair.

Scoring. EASPM is built on CLEP \[[16](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib23 "CLEP-dg: contrastive learning for speech emotion domain generalization via soft prompt tuning")\], a CLAP-based \[[7](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib24 "Clap learning audio concepts from natural language supervision")\] contrastive audio–language encoder fine-tuned on large-scale emotional speech data. Let $`f_{\text{CLEP-A}}(\cdot,t)`$ and $`f_{\text{CLEP-T}}(\cdot)`$ denote the audio and text branches. Using $`\ell_{2}`$-normalized embeddings, the score for candidate $`x_{t-1}^{i}`$ is

``` math
s_{i}\;=\;\Big\langle\frac{f_{\text{CLEP-A}}(x_{t-1}^{i},t)}{\big\|f_{\text{CLEP-A}}(x_{t-1}^{i},t)\big\|_{2}},\,\frac{f_{\text{CLEP-T}}(c)}{\big\|f_{\text{CLEP-T}}(c)\big\|_{2}}\Big\rangle.
```

For a pair $`(x_{t-1}^{w},x_{t-1}^{l})`$ with scores $`s_{w},s_{l}`$, define $`\Delta_{t}=s_{w}-s_{l}`$. The probability that the win item is preferred is modeled by a pairwise logistic with temperature $`\tau>0`$:

``` math
\hat{p}_{w}\;=\;\sigma(\tau\,\Delta_{t})\;=\;\frac{1}{1+\exp(-\tau\,\Delta_{t})}.
```

Candidate selection is performed by $`x_{t-1}^{\text{win}}=\arg\max_{i}s_{i}`$ and $`x_{t-1}^{\text{lose}}=\arg\min_{i}s_{i}`$, and the stepwise preference loss is:

``` math
\mathcal{L}_{\text{pref}}\;=\;-\log\hat{p}_{w}\;=\;\log\!\big(1+\exp(-\tau\,\Delta_{t})\big).
```

Training. EASPM is adapted from CLEP to handle noisy intermediate representations. Given a win–lose pair $`(x_{0}^{w},x_{0}^{l})`$, we sample a timestep $`t`$ and apply the same forward diffusion to produce $`(x_{t}^{w},x_{t}^{l})`$. This tuple $`(x_{t}^{w},x_{t}^{l},t,c)`$ is used to optimize $`\mathcal{L}_{\text{pref}}`$, encouraging the model to recover the correct preference at step $`t`$. A time-aware normalization layer is added to CLEP’s audio branch for timestep conditioning. To reduce mismatch with CLEP’s pretraining domain, we optionally estimate a pseudo-clean $`\hat{x}_{0}`$ from $`x_{t}`$ via deterministic inversion followed by \[[17](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib25 "Denoising diffusion implicit models")\] . After training, EASPM is frozen and used solely as a stepwise scorer for the RL objective.

Random selection of the next state. After EASPM ranks the candidate set $`\{x_{t-1}^{1},\ldots,x_{t-1}^{k}\}`$ and selects a win–lose pair, we *do not* continue with the top sample as shown in Fig. [1](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#S1.F1 "Figure 1 ‣ 1 Introduction ‣ Emotion-Aligned Generation in Diffusion Text to Speech Models via Preference-Guided Optimization"). To avoid biased rollouts and degenerate paths, we uniformly sample the next state $`\tilde{x}_{t-1}`$ from the pool and proceed with $`x_{t-2}\sim p_{\theta}(\cdot\mid\tilde{x}_{t-1},c)`$. This ensures all preference pairs originate from the same latent $`x_{t}`$ while decoupling supervision from sampling. Candidate pooling is applied only when $`t\leq\kappa`$; standard transitions are used for $`t>\kappa`$.

### 2.2 Objective Function of EASPO

We formulate denoising as a $`T`$-step MDP with state $`s_{t}=(c,x_{t})`$, action $`a_{t}=x_{t-1}`$, and policy $`\pi_{\theta}(a_{t}\mid s_{t})=p_{\theta}(x_{t-1}\mid x_{t},c)`$. At each step $`t`$, we sample $`k`$ candidates $`\{x_{t-1}^{i}\}\sim p_{\theta}(\cdot\mid x_{t},c)`$ and rank them via EASPM (Sec. [2.1](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#S2.SS1 "2.1 Emotion-Aware Stepwise Preference Model ‣ 2 Method ‣ Emotion-Aligned Generation in Diffusion Text to Speech Models via Preference-Guided Optimization")). Let $`x_{t-1}^{w}`$ and $`x_{t-1}^{l}`$ denote the top and bottom-ranked samples from the *same* latent $`x_{t}`$.

Dense stepwise reward. EASPM supplies a dense emotional reward at step $`t`$:

``` math
\widehat{R}_{t}^{j}\;=\;s\!\left(x_{t-1}^{j},c,t\right),\qquad\Delta\widehat{R}_{t}\;=\;\widehat{R}_{t}^{w}-\widehat{R}_{t}^{l},
```

Log-likelihood ratio against a reference policy. Let the frozen reference be $`\pi_{\mathrm{ref}}(a_{t}\mid s_{t})=p_{\theta_{\mathrm{ref}}}(x_{t-1}\mid x_{t},c)`$. For $`j\in\{w,l\}`$ we define

``` math
\rho_{t}^{j}(\theta)=\log\pi_{\theta}\!\left(x_{t-1}^{j}\mid s_{t}\right)-\log\pi_{\mathrm{ref}}\!\left(x_{t-1}^{j}\mid s_{t}\right),
```

and use the win–lose difference $`\Delta\rho_{t}=\rho_{t}^{w}(\theta)-\rho_{t}^{l}(\theta)`$ to measure the policy’s preference change relative to the reference.

Stepwise alignment objective. Inspired by \[[3](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib27 "Prdp: proximal reward difference prediction for large-scale reward finetuning of diffusion models")\], we align the log-ratio difference with the dense reward difference via a mean-squared error with a time weight $`\beta_{t}=\lambda^{\,T-t-1}/\eta`$ ($`\lambda\!\in\!(0,1],\,\eta\!>\!0`$):

``` math
\mathcal{L}_{t}(\theta)=\big(\,\beta_{t}\,\Delta\rho_{t}\;-\;\Delta\widehat{R}_{t}\,\big)^{2}.
```

Final EASPO loss. To improve sample efficiency, we optimize at a randomly shuffled step $`\tau`$ and skip the first $`\kappa`$ high-noise steps. Averaging over prompts $`c`$, initial noises $`x_{T}`$, and win/lose pairs from the policy gives

$`\displaystyle\mathcal{L}(\theta)`$$`\displaystyle=\mathbb{E}_{c\sim p(c),x_{T}\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I),\tau\sim\mathcal{U}[1,\,T-\kappa],x_{\tau-1}^{w},\,x_{\tau-1}^{l}\sim p_{\theta}(\cdot\mid x_{\tau},c)}\,`$$`\displaystyle\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\bigg[\Big(\beta_{\tau}\,\big[\rho_{\tau}^{w}(\theta)-\rho_{\tau}^{l}(\theta)\big]\;-\;\big[s(x_{\tau-1}^{w},c,\tau)-s(x_{\tau-1}^{l},c,\tau)\big]\Big)^{2}\bigg],`$

Discussion. Eq. ([7](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#S2.E7 "In 2.2 Objective Function of EASPO ‣ 2 Method ‣ Emotion-Aligned Generation in Diffusion Text to Speech Models via Preference-Guided Optimization")) integrates stepwise preference optimization with reward-difference learning: EASPM provides a *dense emotional reward* (Eq. ([4](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#S2.E4 "In 2.2 Objective Function of EASPO ‣ 2 Method ‣ Emotion-Aligned Generation in Diffusion Text to Speech Models via Preference-Guided Optimization"))), and the diffusion policy is updated so that its *log-likelihood ratio difference* between win/lose transitions (Eq. ([5](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#S2.E5 "In 2.2 Objective Function of EASPO ‣ 2 Method ‣ Emotion-Aligned Generation in Diffusion Text to Speech Models via Preference-Guided Optimization"))) matches that reward difference (Eq. ([6](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#S2.E6 "In 2.2 Objective Function of EASPO ‣ 2 Method ‣ Emotion-Aligned Generation in Diffusion Text to Speech Models via Preference-Guided Optimization"))). Empirical results from \[[3](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib27 "Prdp: proximal reward difference prediction for large-scale reward finetuning of diffusion models")\] show that using reward differences yields more stable reward optimization in diffusion models than standard policy gradient methods.

## 3 Experiments

### 3.1 Datasets and Experimental Setup

We fine-tune EASPM on the English MSP-Podcast corpus \[[12](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib29 "Building naturalistic emotionally balanced speech corpus by retrieving emotional speech from existing podcast recordings")\] ($`\sim`$55k utterances, $`>`$1,200 speakers), using textual prompts from emotion labels and acoustic descriptors (e.g., pitch, loudness, jitter, shimmer). Preference pairs are created by labeling target emotions (e.g., happy) as preferred over distractors (e.g., neutral) with the same text. For reinforcement learning, we use the English split of ESD (5 emotions × 10 speakers, 350 utterances/emotion), with an 8:1:1 train/val/test split per speaker–emotion. We evaluate against seven emotion-controllable TTS baselines: FG‑TTS \[[2](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib1 "Fine-grained style control in transformer-based text-to-speech synthesis")\], PromptTTS \[[10](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib2 "Prompttts: controllable text-to-speech with text descriptions")\], Emospeech \[[4](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib3 "Emospeech: guiding fastspeech2 towards emotional text to speech")\], EmoDiff \[[9](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib4 "Emodiff: intensity controllable emotional text-to-speech with soft-label guidance")\], CosyVoice \[[5](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib5 "Cosyvoice: a scalable multilingual zero-shot text-to-speech synthesizer based on supervised semantic tokens")\], CosyVoice2 \[[6](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib6 "Cosyvoice 2: scalable streaming speech synthesis with large language models")\], and EmoVoice \[[19](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib7 "Emovoice: llm-based emotional text-to-speech model with freestyle text prompting")\], using authors’ code and checkpoints with default inference settings.

### 3.2 Evaluation Metrics

Objective metrics. Emo_SIM quantifies emotional alignment as the average cosine similarity between emotion2vec-base embeddings of generated and reference utterances. Prosod_SIM is computed via AutoPCP \[[1](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib31 "Seamless: multilingual expressive and streaming speech translation")\], which compares utterance-level prosody (rhythm, stress, intonation). WER (word-error-rate) is calculated using Whisper Large-v3 transcripts. UTMOS \[[15](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.25416v2#bib.bib33 "Utmos: utokyo-sarulab system for voicemos challenge 2022")\] is employed to evaluate speech naturalness and perceptual quality.

Subjective metrics. We conduct listening tests with 20 raters. Each system is evaluated on 30 clips per rater (six per emotion across five emotions). MOS assesses naturalness, and Emotion MOS evaluates how well the target emotion is conveyed given the prompt, both on a 1–5 scale (0.5 increments). MOS_EC measures consistency between the generated audio and the instruction (emotion + text). For Emotion Recall, raters identify the perceived emotion from five choices; accuracy is averaged over both utterances and emotion classes.

### 3.3 Main Results

These results demonstrate the effectiveness of our approach in generating emotionally aligned speech with enhanced coherence, while preserving natural prosody and speech quality. Using highest–lowest scoring samples ensures stronger emotional contrast while maintaining comparable content and noise levels, leading to more stable and informative supervision.

## 4 Conclusion

We present EASPO, a diffusion-based speech synthesis framework that introduces stepwise preference optimization for fine-grained emotional alignment. By leveraging an emotion-aware scoring model to compare candidate samples at each denoising step, EASPO progressively guides generation toward emotionally expressive and prosodically natural speech. This step-conditioned training strategy enables the model to capture subtle affective cues through contrastive supervision. Extensive experimental demonstrate the effectiveness across both objective and subjective evaluations.
