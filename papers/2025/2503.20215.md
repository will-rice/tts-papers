# Evaluation

We conduct comprehensive evaluation of . The model is divided into two main categories: understanding (X$`\to`$Text) and speech generation (X$`\to`$Speech).

## Evaluation of X$`\to`$Text

In this section, we evaluate ’s ability to comprehend various multimodal inputs (text, audio, image, and video) and generate textual responses.

#### Text$`\to`$Text

Our evaluation of on text $`\to`$ text primarily focuses on general evaluation, mathematics & science ability and coding ability. Specifically, we utilize MMLU-Pro , MMLU-redux  and Livebench0803  for general evaluation, GPQA , GSM8K  and MATH  for mathematics & science, HumanEval , MBPP , MultiPL-E  and LiveCodeBench 2305-2409  for coding.

#### Audio$`\to`$Text

The evaluation of for audio $`\to`$ text includes audio understanding, audio reasoning, and voice-chatting. Specifically, we perform a comprehensive evaluation on Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR), Speech-to-Text Translation (S2TT), Speech Entity Recognition (SER), Vocal Sound classification (VSC) and Music, which assesses the performance of on a broad range of audio understanding tasks. We utilize MMAU  for audio reasoning tasks, VoiceBench  and a self-curated speech-instruction benchmark for voice-chatting tasks.

#### Image$`\to`$Text

The evaluation of for image $`\to`$ text primarily emphasizes the performance in college-level problems, math, general visual question answering and OCR-related tasks. Specifically, we utilize MMMU  and MMMU-Pro  for college-level problems evaluation, MathVista  and MathVision  for math. For general visual question answering, we evaluate the performance on benchmark datasets such as MMBench-V1.1 , MMVet , MMStar , MME , MuirBench , CRPE , RealWorldQA , MMERealWorld , and MM-MT-Bench . Additionally, we evaluate on various OCR benchmarks, such as AI2D , TextVQA , DocVQA , ChartQA , and OCRBench_v2 . Furthermore, we also evaluate the visual grounding capability of our model on the referring expression comprehension benchmarks , object detection in the wild  and a self-curated point grounding benchmark.

#### Video (w/o Audio)$`\to`$Text

We assess our model on several representative video understanding tasks like Video-MME , MVBench , and EgoSchema .

#### Multimodality$`\to`$Text

We demonstrate the ability of our model for mixed-modality (image, audio and text) prompts on OmniBench .

## Evaluation of X$`\to`$Speech

In this section, we evaluate the speech generation capabilities of . Due to the lack of relevant assessments, the evaluation of speech generation focuses primarily speech generation given texts, similarity to text-to-speech (TTS), on two aspects: Zero-shot and Single-Speaker speech generation capabilities.

- **Zero-Shot Speech Generation** We assessed the content consistency (WER) and speaker similarity (SIM) of our model in zero-shot speech generation on SEED .

- **Single-Speaker Speech Generation** We assessed the stability of our speaker fine-tuned model on the SEED , and evaluated the subjective naturalness (NMOS) of the generated speech on a self-created dataset.

### Evaluation of Zero-Shot Speech Generation.

We compared the with state-of-the-art zero-shot TTS systems. As shown in Table 9, demonstrates highly competitive performance, highlighting its robust speech understanding and generation capabilities developed through in-context learning (ICL). Additionally, after reinforcement learning (RL) optimization, showed significant improvements in generation stability, with marked reductions in attention misalignment, pronunciation errors, and inappropriate pauses on the challenging test-hard dataset.
